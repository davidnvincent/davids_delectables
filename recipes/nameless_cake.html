    • Nanny's Nameless Cake

    Chef's Notes

    If you don't have sour milk or buttermilk, add a little lemon juice to your milk and - voila! - you've got sour milk.
    My mother used to put toasted chopped walnuts in this recipe, but I don't add nuts for the children's birthday cakes.

    Ingredients

    • 1 stick unsalted butter at room temperature
    • 1 stick of margarine
    • 1-1/2 cup granulated sugar
    • 3 eggs at room temperature
    • 1-3/4 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1/2 tsp baking powder
    • 1/2 tsp baking soda
    • 1/2 tsp salt
    • 3/4 tsp nutmeg
    • 1 tsp cinnamon
    • 2 T cocoa
    • 3/4 cup sour milk or buttermilk
    • 1 tsp lemon extract
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract

    Icing:

    • 6 T unsalted butter at room temperature legg yolk
    • 2-3 cups confectioner's sugar
    • 1-1/2T cocoa
    • 1T cinnamon
    • 12T hot strong coffee

    Directions

    1. In an electric mixer, cream the stick of butter, the margarine, the sugar and 3 eggs.
    2. In a separate bowl, sift the dry ingredients - the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, nutmeg, 1tsp cinnamon, and 2 T cocoa.
    3. In yet another bowl, combine the sour milk or buttermilk, and the extracts.
    4. With the electric mixer running, add some of the dry ingredients into the mixer and then some of the buttermilk mixture. Keep alternating until all the ingredients are in the electric mixer and incorporated.
    5. Grease and flour two 8" round or square cake pans. Distribute the batter equally between the two pans.
    6. Bake at 350Â° until a toothpick inserted in the cake comes out dry, about 30 minutes. You might want to check the cake at 25 minutes to see if it's done.
    7. While the cake is baking, make the frosting.
    8. Make a pot of strong hot coffee.
    9. In the cleaned electric mixer, combine the 6 T butter and the egg yolk. Cream well.
    10. Slowly add the confectioner's sugar, the 1-1/2 T cocoa and the 1 T cinnamon. Lastly, add the coffee, a teaspoon at a time until you get the consistency you like. If it's too dry, continue to add more coffee.
    11. When the cake is done, let it rest in the pan on a cooling rack for 5-10 minutes. Then, invert and remove the pans. The cake should come out easily. Let the cakes stand until completely cool, about 30 more minutes.
    12. When the cake is cool, frost the top of one layer. Stack the second layer on top. Frost the top of the second layer. Lastly, frost the sides.
    13. Place on a serving platter and enjoy!
