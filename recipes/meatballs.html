    • Italian Meatballs

    Chef's Notes

    This is one of my most popular hors d'oeuvres - especially with kids. Serve with toothpicks with adults. Another way to use these meatballs is in soups. Before you brown them, drop them in your chicken stock - they'll cook in the broth. Presto! You've just made my version of "Wedding Soup." Also, these meatballs work wonderfully in a red sauce on top of spaghetti.

    Ingredients

    • 1-1/2 lb. ground beef
    • 1/2 lb. ground pork
    • 2 large eggs
    • 2 cups stale bread soaked in milk (or 1-1/2 cups bread crumbs)
    • 1/2 cup parmesan cheese (or pecorino, which is stronger)
    • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
    • 1T salt and pepper
    • 1 tsp garlic salt a dash of allspice
    • 1 whole garlic clove
    • 3T olive oil
    • 3T butter

    Directions

    1. Combine all ingredients except whole garlic clove, olive oil and butter.
    2. Form into small (1-1/4") balls, about the size of a walnut.
    3. Saute the garlic clove in the olive oil and butter. Remove the clove.
    4. Fry meatballs until browned on all sides, about 15-20 minutes, turning once.
